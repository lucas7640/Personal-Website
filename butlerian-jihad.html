June 2026·Dune & AI

The Butlerian Jihad

I am a huge Dune fan, and currently a popular topic of discourse is the Pope's encyclical on AI — properly titled Pope Leo's Magnifica Humanitas. I have not read the Magnifica Humanitas yet, though I am going to prepare to read it by revisiting the Butlerian Jihad from Frank Herbert's Dune. For Dune fans, context here is only going to be from excerpts of book one, Dune.

In the Dune universe, the Butlerian Jihad — or the Great Revolt — is a war on the thinking machines, and was a pivotal point in Dune history. The promise of making the thinking machines was to get rid of slave-like labor; the result of the creation was the continuation of the same power dynamic, only amplified.

The Butlerians declared a Jihad, which could have been called a crusade if not for the Islamic theme of the Dune planet. They defeated the thinking machines in a long and bloody war which started in 201 BG and ended in 108 BG — so 93 years of war. After the war this led to the ban of thinking machines, which continued for the next 10,000 years, to present-time Dune, or the present time of book one: 10,191.

The result of the victory led to a new commandment in the Orange Catholic Bible. In book one, Paul quotes one, saying: "Thou shalt not make a machine in the likeness of a human mind." Paul's mother, in conversation with Paul, says that the Butlerian Jihad took away a crutch from humanity and forced them to develop their minds.

The crutch position, I feel, is happening right now. Much of all writing, coding, and researching is being done by AI. I don't know anyone that hasn't used ChatGPT besides my grandpa — and I think even he's used Gemini unwillingly, because of having a Google phone.

I think everyone is guilty of this crutch, myself included, but it still has not had the dramatic tipping point of singularity. More likely it will be slow and unnoticeable, like the culling that has happened to our attention spans.

I think AI is going to be used no matter what, until we reach a critical point of convergence with the tech — or complete destruction. We probably will see it in our lifetimes, but it won't be like the Butlerian Jihad. I am still very hopeful for our world and the outcome of this AI innovation coming to be. Whatever happens, it will be interesting, and we will overcome it.
